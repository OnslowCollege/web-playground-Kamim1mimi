Finishing touches

This includes: watermark; slight fixes; additional detail. Watermark is for obvious reasons: so other's wont steal it and claim it as theirs. Slight fixes can wary from fixing colours to whole proportions being moved around (it actually happened). The only aditional details I ever add is usually a line that goes around the whole figure to separate them from the background.

Otherwise the background is my last stop. After this, it's best to wait for 30 or more minutes (even if I often don't do so) to help you spot any inconsistencies, mistakes, and anything that your "This image is burned into my memory and all I remember is pixels of light" brain didn't notice. Because of how much you usually stare at the image, it's easy not to notice the mistakes as the image might look vastly different from what it actually is (<-happened to me).

And that is all to my little "tutorial". It was, honestly, really fun to make, and I hope you enjoyed it as much as I did.
