How I draw

Hello, I am Kami. Drawing ever since I was a child, I often found myself thinking: "What if I could just copy someone else? Find out how they do it, and maybe even implore it into my own way of drawing?" You see, humans learn how to everything by copying. Speaking? Copying. Moving? Copying. Drawing? You guessed it, copying It's easier to build a house when you have the ground already there.
Which is why, today, I am going to share my way of drawing, so you can find out how other people draw, and maybe even learn a few things yourself.

Sketch

stuff

Personally, I deem a sketch the most important part of drawing. It determines where everything will go,how it will look, and is the perfect time to give emotion and flow to the art.
Most if not all times, my first sketch is messy and a understandable to only me. A good sketch should show where everything is, how it will look, nd give a feeling. If your sketch doesn't have a feeling (unless you're using colours for that), it won't have it at all. Sketching is where you let yourself be free and do whatever you want untill you settle for one you like.

Sometimes I also draw the outline, a silluete, and kidna.. sculpt it? Slowly add details, one bit by another, untill I have a sketch. Or I simply draw the silluete and then draw the more detailed sketch afterwards.

Detailed sketch

This is where I add clothing, hair, expression and over all more detailed character.

Optional!
if you want your art to have colors, it's best to color the image (messily) while still in the sketching phase. I, however, don't do it often because I'm lazy or already have a colored character.
